# CrickCoach AI — Funding Plan

**Question:** "I want to apply for funding — which type of investor is best?"

**Short answer:** For your current stage (live app, a few paying users, no strong growth metrics yet), the best investors are **angels, accelerators, and pre-seed micro-VCs** — *not* the big institutional VCs. Raise a **smaller pre-seed round now**, hit growth milestones, then raise the $1M+ round in 12–18 months.

---

## 1. The honest framing: your ask vs. your stage

You said you want to raise **$1M+ (₹8Cr+)** while being **early / pre-revenue**. These don't match, and it's important to know that before you start pitching.

A $1M+ round normally requires *one* of:
- A **repeat or well-known founder** (already had an exit / big-company pedigree), **or**
- **Strong metrics** — real revenue growing month over month, with users who stick, **or**
- A **hot deeptech wedge** investors are racing to back.

As a solo, first-time founder who's pre-revenue, pitching big VCs for $1M+ today mostly produces *"come back when you have traction."* Worse, a "no" from a top fund is hard to reverse later — you often only get one shot at their attention.

**The right move:** raise a **pre-seed round of ~$150K–$400K (₹1.25–3.5Cr)** now. That funds the hires in `HIRING_PLAN.md` (start with the senior backend engineer) plus ~12–18 months of runway — enough to produce the metrics that make a $1M+ round fundable.

---

## 2. Best-fit investor types — ranked for *your* stage

### 1. Angel investors / operator angels  ← **start here**
Individuals writing **$10K–$50K** checks. They decide fast and back the **founder + product**, not spreadsheets — perfect for pre-revenue. Target angels who:
- love cricket / play it, or
- have built **consumer mobile apps** or **sports-tech**, or
- have operator backgrounds (ex-founders, senior product/engineering people).

You'll typically stack **5–15 angels** to fill the round.

### 2. Accelerators
For a solo founder these give the most *besides* money: **credibility, structure, mentorship, and an investor network** for the next round.
- **India:** 100X.VC, Antler India, Indian Angel Network accelerator, JioGenNext, T-Hub.
- **Global / sports-tech:** Techstars, Y Combinator (long shot but worth a shot — global), Stadia Ventures and other sports-tech accelerators.

### 3. Angel networks / syndicates
Platforms that aggregate many small checks into one round — efficient way to fill a pre-seed:
- Indian Angel Network, Mumbai Angels, LetsVenture, AngelList India syndicates.

### 4. Pre-seed / micro-VCs
Small funds that will **lead** a pre-seed round (write the anchor check others follow):
- 100X.VC, Better Capital, First Cheque, Java Capital, All In Capital.

### 5. Strategic / sports angels  ← **high leverage for your niche**
Ex-cricketers, cricket academy owners, coaches, sports-brand operators. They bring **money + distribution + credibility** inside the exact market you serve. One well-known cricket name on your cap table is worth far more than the cheque size suggests.

---

## 3. Non-dilutive options (money without giving up equity)

- **Government / startup grants:** Startup India Seed Fund Scheme (SISFS), state startup grants, BIRAC / deeptech grants. Slow and paperwork-heavy, but it's **free money + validation** that doesn't dilute you.
- **Your own revenue:** you already have paying users — pushing **monthly revenue up** is the cheapest "funding" there is, and it's the single metric every later investor will ask about. Every paying user now de-risks the next raise.

---

## 4. Who NOT to approach yet (and why)

Large institutional seed / Series-A funds — **Peak XV (ex-Sequoia India), Accel, Blume, Lightspeed, etc. at their main funds.** They expect traction you don't have yet. Pitching now usually earns a "no," and that closes a door you'll want open in 12–18 months when you *do* have the metrics. Build the relationship early if you can, but don't formally pitch them for the cheque yet.

---

## 5. The path to the $1M+ round

Use the pre-seed money to hit the milestones that unlock the big round:

1. **Revenue run-rate** — a real, growing monthly number (even small is fine if it's *growing*).
2. **Month-over-month growth %** — the headline VCs care about most.
3. **Retention** — users who keep coming back and keep paying (proves the product works).
4. **A team in place** — execute `HIRING_PLAN.md` (backend engineer first, then mobile, then R&D/ML). A funded solo founder with no team is a red flag to seed VCs.
5. **Proven AI accuracy** — credible evidence your analysis (ball-speed, bat-ball contact, pose feedback) is actually accurate. This is your moat; ties directly to the R&D / ML hire.

Hit these and the $1M+ round goes from "unfundable" to a natural next step.

---

## 6. What to prepare before approaching anyone

- **Pitch deck** (10–12 slides): problem, product/demo, market, traction, business model, team, the ask, use of funds.
- **One-page summary** to send cold (the "teaser").
- **Basic metrics dashboard:** users, paying users, revenue, retention, growth.
- **Cap table** (who owns what today — clean and simple).
- **Demo video:** the app analyzing a real cricket video, start to finish. For a CV product this is your strongest single asset — investors need to *see* it work.

> `COMPANY_DESCRIPTION.md` already covers the business overview, value prop, and target market — reuse it as raw material for the deck and one-pager.

## Suggested first moves (in order)

1. Push current revenue and clean up basic metrics (this week — costs nothing, raises your value).
2. Build the deck + demo video using `COMPANY_DESCRIPTION.md`.
3. Apply to **1–2 accelerators** (100X.VC, Antler India) — money + network + credibility in one move.
4. In parallel, line up **5–15 angels**, prioritizing cricket / sports / consumer-app people.
5. Apply for **Startup India Seed Fund** (non-dilutive, runs in the background).
6. Target a **pre-seed of ~$150K–$400K**, not $1M+. Go for the big round once Section 5's milestones are real.
